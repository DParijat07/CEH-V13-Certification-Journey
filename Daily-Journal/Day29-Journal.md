# Day 29 Journal

**Date:** 24/06/2026

## Summary

Today I attended my 8th CEH training class at IEMLabs. The session focused on understanding the fundamentals of **Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) attacks**, one of the most common network-based attack techniques. I learned how attackers can position themselves between two communicating systems to intercept and monitor network traffic without the knowledge of either party.

The class also introduced **Ettercap**, a network security tool commonly used for packet sniffing, traffic analysis, and demonstrating MITM attacks through techniques such as ARP spoofing. The session provided a foundational understanding of how network interception attacks work and why secure communication protocols are important.

After the theory session, I worked through the **WiFi Hacking 101** room on TryHackMe. The room covered the basics of wireless networking, including concepts such as SSID, BSSID, access points, channels, and wireless authentication mechanisms. I explored the differences between open networks, WPA/WPA2 security, and WPA3, gaining a better understanding of wireless security fundamentals.
