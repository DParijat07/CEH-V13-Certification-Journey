# CEH Module 01 - Introduction to Ethical Hacking

## What is Ethical Hacking?

Ethical Hacking is the authorized process of identifying, testing, and reporting security vulnerabilities in systems, networks, and applications to improve their security.

### Goals

* Identify vulnerabilities before attackers do
* Assess security posture
* Improve security controls
* Reduce organizational risk

---

# Information Security Fundamentals

## Information Security (InfoSec)

Information Security is the practice of protecting information from unauthorized access, modification, disclosure, destruction, or disruption.

### CIA Triad

### 1. Confidentiality

Ensures only authorized users can access information.

Examples:

* Encryption
* Access Control
* Password Protection

### 2. Integrity

Ensures data remains accurate and unaltered.

Examples:

* Hashing
* Digital Signatures
* Checksums

### 3. Availability

Ensures systems and data are accessible when needed.

Examples:

* Backups
* Redundancy
* Disaster Recovery

---

# Cybersecurity vs Information Security

| Information Security     | Cybersecurity           |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------- |
| Protects all information | Protects digital assets |
| Physical and digital     | Mainly digital          |
| Broader concept          | Subset of InfoSec       |

---

# Key Security Concepts

## Asset

Anything valuable to an organization.

Examples:

* Servers
* Databases
* Customer Data
* Applications

## Threat

Anything capable of causing harm.

Examples:

* Malware
* Hackers
* Insider Threats
* Natural Disasters

## Vulnerability

A weakness that can be exploited.

Examples:

* Weak Passwords
* Unpatched Systems
* Misconfigurations

## Risk

Likelihood of a threat exploiting a vulnerability.

### Formula

Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact

# Types of Hackers

## White Hat Hacker

Authorized security professional.

Purpose:

* Improve security
* Conduct penetration tests

Examples:

* Penetration Tester
* Security Consultant

---

## Black Hat Hacker

Unauthorized attacker.

Purpose:

* Theft
* Fraud
* Disruption

---

## Gray Hat Hacker

Acts without permission but usually not for malicious purposes.

---

## Script Kiddie

Uses tools created by others with limited technical knowledge.

---

## Hacktivist

Uses hacking to promote political or social causes.

---

## State-Sponsored Hacker

Works for governments or intelligence agencies.

---

# Cyber Threats

## Malware

Malicious software designed to damage systems.

### Types

* Virus
* Worm
* Trojan
* Ransomware
* Spyware
* Rootkit

---

## Phishing

Fraudulent attempt to steal sensitive information.

Types:

* Email Phishing
* Spear Phishing
* Whaling
* Smishing
* Vishing

---

## Denial of Service (DoS)

Attempts to make a service unavailable.

### DDoS

Distributed Denial of Service using multiple systems.

---

## Social Engineering

Manipulating people into revealing sensitive information.

Examples:

* Pretexting
* Baiting
* Tailgating
* Impersonation

# Ethical Hacking Methodology

## 1. Reconnaissance

Gather information about the target.

Examples:

* WHOIS
* Google Dorking
* Social Media Analysis

---

## 2. Scanning & Enumeration

Identify systems, ports, and services.

Tools:

* Nmap
* Netcat

---

## 3. Vulnerability Analysis

Identify weaknesses.

Tools:

* Nessus
* OpenVAS

---

## 4. Exploitation

Attempt to gain access.

Tools:

* Metasploit
* Burp Suite

---

## 5. Post Exploitation

Actions performed after compromise.

Examples:

* Privilege Escalation
* Credential Access
* Persistence

---

## 6. Reporting

Document findings and remediation steps.

Report Includes:

* Vulnerability Details
* Risk Rating
* Evidence
* Recommendations

---

# Penetration Testing

Penetration Testing is a controlled attack simulation performed to identify vulnerabilities.

### Objectives

* Discover weaknesses
* Validate security controls
* Measure risk exposure

---

# Vulnerability Assessment vs Penetration Testing

| Vulnerability Assessment | Penetration Testing      |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------ |
| Finds vulnerabilities    | Exploits vulnerabilities |
| Broad coverage           | Deep testing             |
| Lower risk               | Higher risk              |
| Automated + Manual       | Primarily Manual         |
