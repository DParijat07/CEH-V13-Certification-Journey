# Introduction to Social Engineering

## Overview

Social Engineering is the practice of manipulating individuals into revealing sensitive information, granting unauthorized access, or performing actions that compromise security.

Unlike technical attacks that exploit software vulnerabilities, social engineering attacks exploit human behavior and psychology.

---

## Definition

Social Engineering is a psychological manipulation technique used by attackers to influence individuals into making security mistakes or disclosing confidential information.

---

## Why Social Engineering is Effective

Organizations often invest heavily in:

* Firewalls
* Antivirus Solutions
* Intrusion Detection Systems
* Security Monitoring

However, attackers frequently target people because humans can be easier to manipulate than technical systems.

---

## Real-World Example

```text
Secure Network
      ↓
Employee Receives Phishing Email
      ↓
Employee Clicks Link
      ↓
Credentials Stolen
      ↓
Network Compromised
```

---

## Common Objectives of Social Engineering Attacks

Attackers typically aim to:

* Steal credentials
* Gain unauthorized access
* Install malware
* Collect sensitive information
* Commit financial fraud
* Conduct espionage
* Obtain confidential documents

---

## Types of Information Targeted

Examples include:

* Usernames
* Passwords
* Banking Information
* Credit Card Details
* Personal Information
* Corporate Documents
* Authentication Tokens

---

## Social Engineering Attack Categories

### Human-Based Attacks

Direct interaction with victims.

Examples:

* Impersonation
* Phone Calls
* Physical Access Attempts

### Computer-Based Attacks

Technology-assisted attacks.

Examples:

* Phishing Emails
* Fake Websites
* Pop-Up Messages
* Social Media Scams

---

## Common Attack Vectors

* Email
* Phone Calls
* SMS Messages
* Social Media
* USB Devices
* Fake Websites
* Physical Interaction

---

## Why Security Professionals Study Social Engineering

Understanding social engineering helps:

* Identify attack attempts
* Protect users
* Improve awareness programs
* Strengthen organizational security
* Reduce human-related security risks

---

## Key Takeaways

* Social Engineering targets people rather than systems.
* Human behavior is often the weakest security control.
* Attackers use trust, fear, urgency, and curiosity.
* Awareness and verification are essential defenses.
* Social Engineering remains one of the most successful attack methods worldwide.
